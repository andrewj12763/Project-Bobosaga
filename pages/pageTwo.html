Calorie Diet

Calorie Basics Choosing to start a diet plan that involves counting calories is a simple, basic way to lose weight. However, often dieters are not sure of the number of calories they should consume to achieve successful weight loss. The key to losing weight is to take in fewer calories, or burn more calories, than your body needs, creating a calorie deficit. The base metabolic rate that you just calculated is what you need to maintaine your weight or the starting point for the math.

Each pound is the equivalent of 3500 calories. To lose approximately one pound per week you would need to reduce your caloric intake by 500 calories each day, or reduce it by 250 calories and exercise enough to burn the additional 250 calories. Dieters are often not sure which calorie based plan is best for them. Many arbitrarily pick a diet plan they think has the correct number of calories for them such as a 1200 or 1500 calorie plan. This is generally not the best way to decide how many calories your body needs daily to receive proper nutrition and remain healthy. The decision should be based on your current weight and activity level.

A standard diet would be to reduce your intake by 250 calories a day and excersize to burn 250 calories.
